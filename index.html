Biography

Biography

Josephine Adelegan is a PhD candidate and graduate researcher in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at Howard University, advised by Dr. Kimberly Jones and Dr. Sanjib Sharma. Her research sits at the critical intersection of environmental engineering and artificial intelligence, focusing on the safe and efficient remediation of high-level radioactive waste at legacy sites such as the Hanford Site.

Deeply influenced by her Nigerian heritage, Josephine developed an early and profound understanding of the need for resilient, sustainable water systems. She develops advanced, physics-informed machine learning frameworks that integrate thermodynamic principles with time-lagged algorithmic inputs to reduce data leakage and model the true physical behavior of crossflow ultrafiltration systems.

Beyond her technical research, Josephine is active in scientific leadership and mentorship. She serves as a committee member and Field Chair for the CRESP ECP committee, is a recognized AIAS CRIT Scholar, and recently served as a STEM Ambassador at the Waste Management Symposium, where she presented her predictive methodologies for environmental systems.
